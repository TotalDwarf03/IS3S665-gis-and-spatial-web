Las Vegas

Las Vegas, often referred to as "Vegas," is a vibrant city located in the state of Nevada, USA. It is renowned for its bustling nightlife, world-class entertainment, and luxurious casinos. The city is situated in the Mojave Desert and is known for its hot desert climate, with scorching summers and mild winters. Las Vegas is a major tourist destination, attracting millions of visitors each year who come to experience its famous Strip, which is lined with elaborate themed hotels, casinos, restaurants, and entertainment venues. In addition to gambling, Las Vegas offers a variety of attractions including live shows, fine dining, shopping, and outdoor activities such as hiking in nearby Red Rock Canyon. The city also hosts numerous conventions and events, making it a hub for business and leisure alike.

Coordinates: 36.1699° N, 115.1398° W

Population: Approximately 641,000 (as of 2020)

Area: 135.8 square miles (351.7 km²)

Famous Landmarks: The Las Vegas Strip, Bellagio Fountains, Fremont Street Experience, The Venetian, Stratosphere Tower

Notable Events: Electric Daisy Carnival, World Series of Poker, Las Vegas Film Festival, CES (Consumer Electronics Show)

Major Industries: Tourism, Hospitality, Entertainment, Gaming, Conventions

Climate: Hot desert climate with extremely hot summers and mild winters

Fun Fact: Las Vegas is home to more than 150,000 hotel rooms, making it one of the largest hotel markets in the world.
