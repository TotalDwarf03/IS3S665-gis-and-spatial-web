Los Angeles

Los Angeles, often abbreviated as L.A., is the largest city in California and the second-largest city in the United States. It is located in Southern California and is known for its Mediterranean climate, diverse culture, and sprawling metropolitan area. Los Angeles is a major center for the entertainment industry, particularly Hollywood, which is home to many film studios and celebrities. The city is also known for its beaches, such as Venice Beach and Santa Monica, as well as landmarks like the Hollywood Sign, Griffith Observatory, and the Getty Center. Los Angeles has a rich history, with influences from Native American, Spanish, Mexican, and American cultures. The city is a hub for business, international trade, technology, and fashion.

Coordinates: 34.0522° N, 118.2437° W

Population: Approximately 4 million (as of 2020)

Area: 503 square miles (1,302 km²)

Famous Landmarks: Hollywood Sign, Griffith Observatory, Getty Center, Santa Monica Pier, Venice Beach

Notable Events: Academy Awards (Oscars), LA Film Festival, LA Pride Parade, Rose Parade

Major Industries: Entertainment, Technology, Fashion, International Trade, Tourism

Climate: Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers

Fun Fact: Los Angeles is home to the largest historical theater district on the National Register of Historic Places, known as the Broadway Theater District.
