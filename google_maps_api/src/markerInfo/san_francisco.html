San Francisco

San Francisco, officially the City and County of San Francisco, is a city in the U.S. state of California, located on the West Coast. It is the cultural, commercial, and financial center of Northern California. San Francisco is known for its iconic landmarks such as the Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, and its historic cable cars. The city is also famous for its diverse neighborhoods, vibrant arts scene, and progressive culture. San Francisco has a rich history, including its role in the California Gold Rush and its significance in the LGBTQ+ rights movement. The city is a major hub for technology and innovation, with many tech companies headquartered in the nearby Silicon Valley.

Coordinates: 37.7749° N, 122.4194° W

Population: Approximately 815,000 (as of 2020)

Area: 47.35 square miles (122.4 km²)

Famous Landmarks: Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, Fisherman's Wharf, Chinatown, Lombard Street

Notable Events: San Francisco Pride Parade, Outside Lands Music and Arts Festival, Chinese New Year Parade

Major Industries: Technology, Finance, Tourism, Biotechnology

Climate: Mediterranean climate with mild, wet winters and dry summers

Fun Fact: San Francisco is known for its steep hills, with some streets having gradients as steep as 31.5%.
